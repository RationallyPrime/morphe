# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Morphe serves two closely related audiences. The primary audience is the operator who must read
dense operational truth, recognize state and risk, follow lineage, and move through a task without
decoding the underlying system. The secondary audience is the consumer team that authors typed
surface models and embeds Morphe as its presentation substrate. The operator's comprehension wins
whenever those audiences' needs compete; implementation detail must not leak into the rendered
experience merely because it is convenient for the producer.

## Product Purpose

Morphe turns typed structure into perceivable, trustworthy interfaces. It gives people, compilers,
and models a closed capability grammar with an open compound vocabulary, then renders their trees
through a compositional context algebra and swappable dialects. Success means the same authored
meaning remains legible, accessible, and operationally useful across hosts, data shapes, adaptive
choices, and visual identities without coupling the tree to pixels, colors, framework internals,
or live host state.

## Brand Personality

Calm, exact, trustworthy. Morphe should feel intellectually serious without becoming austere,
technically powerful without exposing machinery for its own sake, and distinctive without visual
spectacle. Dense generated output should feel deliberately edited and composed rather than dumped
from a schema.

## Anti-references

Morphe must not resemble a generic SaaS dashboard, a wall of interchangeable metric cards, a
pretty-printed API response, or a terminal theme applied indiscriminately to business data. It must
avoid decorative glass, ambient shadows, gradient text, ornamental charts, tiny low-contrast
metadata, raw identifiers promoted to primary content, and dialects that preserve nominal token
coverage while sacrificing readability. It must also avoid consumer branding, workflows, and
product strategy leaking into the package substrate.

## Design Principles

1. **Operational truth before decoration.** Reading order, state, consequence, and next action must
   be obvious before a dialect adds character.
2. **Calm is structural.** Context, density, spacing, and bounded emphasis create hierarchy; card
   proliferation and chromatic noise do not.
3. **Meaning survives every dialect.** A valid tree must preserve legibility, focus, status, and
   affordance under every shipped intent mapping.
4. **Generated surfaces deserve editorial judgment.** Compilers and presenters should select,
   group, label, and progressively disclose information so output feels authored.
5. **Open vocabulary, closed capability.** Reusable compounds should expand Morphe's expressive
   range without smuggling application logic, host state, or new browser authority into data.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 Level AA across every shipped dialect and supported viewport. Text and interactive
states must meet contrast requirements on their actual composed surfaces; color may never be the
only signal. Keyboard order, visible focus, native semantics, labelled controls, reduced motion,
zoom, narrow layouts, and comprehensible failure states are release requirements. Dense operational
views must remain usable for people with low vision, color-vision differences, motor constraints,
and limited working memory.
